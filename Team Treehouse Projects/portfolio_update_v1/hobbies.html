Hobbies

I believe hobbies are an important form of self-expression and what we do in our free time tells the world alot about who we are. While I have many hobbies, here are my three favorite hobbies:

  1. Gardening
  2. Baking
  3. Learning to Code

Gardening

I began gardening in 2016 when I lived on an 80 acre farm in East Texas. I have never been interested in growing flowers, however. I love growing food! I didn't know anyone who knew how to garden so I taught myself using youtube videos. Gardening has been very rewarding because you can see and enjoy the "fruits" of your labor.

Baking

I began baking bread around the same time that I was learning to garden. Being at home allowed me extra time to explore interests that I had always been too busy to try while I worked outside the home. The first thing I learned to bake (and still my favorite thing to bake) is bread. I was very intimidated and overwhelmed at first. But once I got the hang of it, I realized it was quite easy. Now, I love trying out new bread recipes and adding all kinds of fun ingredients to make ordinary bread EXTRAORDINARY!

Learning to Code

Learning to code has been in the back of my mind for years. I remember growing up in the 1990's as the computer world entered homes across America. I also remember taking my first computer science class in 1999. My mind was blown and I was out of that class as fast as I could! I never looked back. I assumed computers weren't for me and I pursued my degree in education. I then went on to become a teacher. However, the one thing I learned by creating my own small, online business was that I was capable of much more than I believed in 1999.
